On "the early lessons feel simple / can we get there faster": that's by design for a 9–13-year-old's first hour — World 1 is deliberately blind sequencing + loops with no sensing or reacting, so nothing she learns needs unlearning. But the ramp is a dial we can turn. Three ways to steepen it without breaking the ladder:
• Bonus "★★★ hard" stars — an optional advanced goal per mission (fewest lines, a loop where a copy-paste would do) for kids who want more.
• Compress the on-ramp — fold M2–M3 into fewer, denser missions so repeat, sensing, and variables arrive a world or two sooner.
• A "fast track" — let a confident kid test out of a world's practices straight to its boss.
